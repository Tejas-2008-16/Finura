Loan processing fees and charges: look beyond the rate

Interest is the largest cost for most loans, but upfront and conditional charges can still change the value of an offer.

What is a processing fee?

A processing fee is a charge a lender may collect for reviewing and setting up a loan application. The amount, tax treatment and refundability vary. Ask whether it is deducted from the disbursed amount or collected separately, because that changes how much money reaches you.

Charges worth asking about

  • Processing, documentation, valuation or legal charges.
  • Insurance or add-on products included in the offer.
  • Late-payment, bounce and repayment-mode charges.
  • Part-prepayment, foreclosure or conversion fees, where applicable.

Compare the full offer in writing

Put competing offers into the same notes: loan amount, annual rate, tenure, EMI, total interest and each fee. Then use the calculator to test comparable rates and tenures. A slightly lower EMI can still come with a higher total cost.

Ask before signing: Request a breakdown of every charge and a copy of the repayment schedule. If a fee is unclear, get a written explanation from the lender.

Do not finance a surprise

Some costs are due before disbursement, while others may be added to the loan. Know the timing so you have enough cash to complete the transaction without taking on extra short-term debt.
