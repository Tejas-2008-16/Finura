Before you sign

A loan repayment checklist for calmer decisions

A few deliberate checks before signing can prevent confusion after disbursement and help you choose a loan you can sustain.

1. Confirm the amount you will actually receive

Compare the sanctioned amount with the amount that will be credited after any upfront deductions. If the difference matters to your plan, ask whether the charge can be paid separately or reconsider the amount requested.

2. Check the EMI, date and first-payment rule

Verify the exact EMI, repayment start date, due date and repayment method. Ask how the first or partial-period interest is handled. Save the official schedule and compare its core figures with your estimate from the EMI calculator.

3. Read the flexibility terms

  • Can you make a part-prepayment, and are there limits or charges?
  • Will a prepayment reduce the EMI, the tenure, or allow you to choose?
  • What late fees apply if a payment is delayed?
  • For a floating rate, how will future changes affect the EMI or tenure?

4. Protect your cash flow

Set up reminders before the due date and keep the repayment account funded. More importantly, retain an emergency buffer after your down payment or upfront charges. An affordable loan should not make routine surprises unmanageable.

Keep a record: Save the sanction letter, repayment schedule, fee breakdown and confirmation of every prepayment in one secure place.
