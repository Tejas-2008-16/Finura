Home loan EMI: a practical guide

Use the monthly payment to plan, but compare the total cost before you choose a lender or tenure.

What makes up a home loan EMI?

Each EMI contains interest on the outstanding balance and a principal repayment. Early payments are interest-heavy; over time, more of each payment reduces the principal. A standard reducing-balance calculation is used for most Indian home loans.

Choose a tenure with both costs in view

A longer tenure lowers the monthly payment, which can make a budget more comfortable. It also keeps more principal outstanding for longer and usually increases total interest. Test a few tenures in the EMI calculator before applying.

Compare offers fairly

  • Compare the annual interest rate and whether it is fixed or floating.
  • Ask about processing charges, insurance, and legal or valuation fees.
  • Check part-prepayment and foreclosure terms in writing.
  • Keep an emergency fund rather than putting every rupee into the down payment.
Planning tip: A lower EMI is not automatically a cheaper loan. Compare total interest and the flexibility to prepay.

Can prepayments help?

Yes. A prepayment reduces the balance on which future interest is calculated. It is generally most effective earlier in the loan, subject to your lender's terms and your own cash-flow needs. Read our prepayment guide for a fuller explanation.
