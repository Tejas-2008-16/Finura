5 Proven Strategies to Reduce Your Outstanding Loan Interest

By Emma Lind • Published on July 04, 2026 • 7 Min Read

1. The 13th EMI Annual Strategy

One of the easiest ways to significantly shorten your loan term is to make one extra full monthly payment every calendar year. By paying 13 EMIs in a 12-month period, you reduce the outstanding principal balance directly.

For a typical 30-year home mortgage at 6%, this single extra payment per year reduces your overall repayment timeline by approximately 4 to 5 years, saving you tens of thousands of dollars in cumulative interest.

2. The "Round Up" Payment Trick

If your monthly EMI is $943, consider rounding it up to $1,000 every month. That extra $57 may seem small, but because it goes entirely toward buying down the principal, it has a compounding savings effect over 15 to 30 years.

"Consistently adding small, rounded amounts to your monthly payment is a low-stress way to achieve financial freedom ahead of schedule."

3. Shorter Tenure vs. Refinancing

When interest rates in the market drop, many borrowers refinance to lower their monthly EMI. However, a smarter interest-saving strategy is to refinance to a shorter tenure (e.g. from 30 years down to 15 years) or keep your monthly payment the same as it was before the rate drop.

Lowering your rate while keeping payments high accelerates principal paydown, maximizing interest savings.

4. Renegotiating with Your Current Lender

Refinancing with a new bank involves closing costs and legal fees. Before you switch lenders, contact your current bank. If you have an excellent payment history and credit score, they may offer to match the competitor’s rate for a nominal processing fee, saving you upfront costs.

5. Channeling Windfalls to Principal

Make it a rule to allocate a portion of any unexpected financial windfalls—such as tax refunds, work bonuses, or inheritance—directly to your loan principal.

Our prepayment simulator shows that even a single $5,000 one-time prepayment in Year 3 of a $100,000 mortgage yields much larger long-term interest savings than the same prepayment made in Year 15. The earlier you prepay, the more time the savings have to compound.

6. Reduction FAQs

Does prepaying a loan reduce the monthly EMI?
When making a bulk prepayment, most banks ask if you want to reduce your monthly EMI or reduce your loan tenure. Reducing tenure saves you significantly more money in cumulative interest.
When is the best time to prepay a loan?
The best time to prepay is as early in the loan tenure as possible. Because interest is charged on the outstanding principal, reducing the principal in the early years prevents interest from compounding.
Is it better to invest extra cash or prepay a mortgage?
It depends on your loan interest rate. If your mortgage rate is 3% and the stock market returns 7%, investing the cash is mathematically superior. If your mortgage rate is 7%, prepaying guarantees a risk-free 7% return on your money.
