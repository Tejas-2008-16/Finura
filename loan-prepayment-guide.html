Borrowing strategy

Loan prepayment explained

Paying extra toward principal can reduce interest, particularly early in a reducing-balance loan.

What a prepayment changes

A part-prepayment lowers the outstanding principal. Your lender may reduce the EMI, shorten the tenure, or offer a choice. Reducing tenure often preserves the existing monthly budget and can reduce more interest, but ask the lender for a revised schedule.

Before making a prepayment

  • Check whether charges or minimum amounts apply.
  • Keep emergency savings intact.
  • Ask for written confirmation that the payment is applied to principal.
  • Request an updated repayment schedule.

Timing matters

Interest is calculated on the balance. Therefore, an earlier eligible prepayment generally has longer to reduce future interest. It is still wise to weigh other high-interest debt and financial priorities first.
