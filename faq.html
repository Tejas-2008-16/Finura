EMI basics

What does EMI stand for?

Equated Monthly Installment — a fixed monthly payment made up of principal and interest, paid until the loan is fully repaid.

How is EMI calculated?

Using the reducing-balance formula EMI = P × r × (1+r)ⁿ ÷ [(1+r)ⁿ − 1], where P is principal, r is the monthly interest rate, and n is the tenure in months. See our full explanation.

Is EMI the same every month?

Yes, the EMI amount stays fixed for the tenure of a fixed-rate loan. What changes month to month is the split between principal and interest within that fixed payment.

What is the difference between EMI and interest rate?

The interest rate is the annual cost of borrowing, expressed as a percentage. EMI is the actual rupee amount you pay each month, which is derived from the interest rate, principal and tenure.

Can EMI change during the loan tenure?

On a fixed-rate loan, no. On a floating-rate loan, your EMI or tenure can change if the lender's benchmark rate moves. See fixed vs floating rates.

Loan amount, rate & tenure

Does a higher down payment reduce EMI?

Yes. A larger down payment lowers the principal you borrow, which directly reduces both your EMI and the total interest you'll pay.

Does a longer tenure always cost more?

You pay less per month, but typically more in total interest, since interest accrues on the outstanding balance for a longer period.

What is a good loan tenure?

The shortest tenure whose EMI you can comfortably afford without straining your monthly budget. See our tenure guide for a structured way to decide.

Why do two lenders quote different EMIs for the same loan amount?

Differences usually come from the interest rate offered, processing fees, and how each lender rounds or compounds interest.

What is a moratorium period?

A period, common in education loans, during which you aren't required to make full EMI payments — often covering your study period plus a few months after.

Prepayment & foreclosure

Does prepayment reduce EMI or tenure?

Most lenders let you choose. Reducing tenure while keeping EMI the same typically saves more total interest than reducing the EMI. See our prepayment guide.

Is there a penalty for prepaying a loan?

For floating-rate loans to individuals, Indian regulations generally prohibit prepayment penalties. Fixed-rate loans and some business loans may still carry a charge — check your loan agreement.

What is loan foreclosure?

Paying off the entire outstanding balance in one payment before the scheduled tenure ends, closing the loan early.

Is it better to prepay early in the loan?

Generally yes. Because interest is front-loaded on a reducing-balance loan, prepaying earlier removes more future interest than prepaying the same amount later.

Credit score & eligibility

Does my credit score affect my EMI?

Indirectly, yes. A higher credit score often qualifies you for a lower interest rate, which reduces your EMI for the same loan amount. See our credit score guide.

What credit score do I need for a loan?

Requirements vary by lender and loan type, but most lenders prefer a score of 700 or above for the best rates.

Does applying for multiple loans hurt my credit score?

Multiple hard inquiries in a short period can lower your score slightly, since it may signal higher credit risk to lenders.

What other factors affect loan eligibility besides credit score?

Income, existing debt obligations, employment stability, and the loan-to-value ratio for secured loans all factor into a lender's decision. See our eligibility factors guide.

General borrowing questions

What is the difference between a secured and unsecured loan?

A secured loan (like a home or car loan) is backed by collateral, usually resulting in a lower interest rate. An unsecured loan (like a personal loan) has no collateral and typically carries a higher rate.

What is a co-applicant?

A second person who applies for the loan alongside you and shares responsibility for repayment, often used to improve eligibility or combine incomes.

What is loan-to-value ratio?

The percentage of an asset's value that a lender is willing to finance. A lower ratio typically means a larger down payment is required from you.

How much of my income should go toward EMIs?

A common guideline is keeping total EMI obligations under 40% of your monthly take-home income, though comfortable limits vary by individual circumstances.

What happens if I miss an EMI payment?

You'll typically face late fees, a negative mark on your credit report, and, if missed repeatedly, risk of default proceedings depending on the loan type and lender policy.
