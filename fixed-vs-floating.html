Fixed vs floating interest rates

The best option depends on the loan terms, your budget and your comfort with changing payments.

Fixed rate

A fixed rate is intended to remain unchanged for a defined period or the loan term, subject to the agreement. It makes budgeting easier because the repayment amount is more predictable. Check the fine print: some products are fixed only for an initial period.

Floating rate

A floating rate can move with the lender's benchmark and policy. Your EMI or tenure may change after a rate revision. It may work well if rates fall, but your budget should absorb an increase too.

Compare the terms, not the label

  • What benchmark and spread apply to a floating rate?
  • How and when will the lender communicate revisions?
  • Is a fixed rate higher, and for how long is it fixed?
  • Are switching, prepayment or conversion charges applicable?
Use scenarios: calculate your EMI at today's rate and at a modestly higher rate. The difference shows the budget cushion you need.
