Loan eligibility: how lenders assess an application

An approval decision is not based on one number alone. It is a practical assessment of whether the loan fits your income, existing commitments and the lender’s policy.

What lenders commonly review

Every lender has its own process, but applications are commonly reviewed using income evidence, employment or business stability, existing EMIs, credit history, the amount requested and the loan type. A strong point in one area does not guarantee approval; the overall picture matters.

Start with your monthly capacity

List your regular take-home income and essential spending, then include every existing EMI and card repayment. The space left over is more useful than a headline loan amount because it shows whether a new EMI will remain manageable through an ordinary month.

Practical check: Use the EMI calculator with a slightly higher interest rate as well as the rate you expect. This gives your budget a sensible cushion.

Prepare clear, consistent information

  • Keep salary, tax or business-income records current and easy to verify.
  • Check the details on your credit report and dispute genuine errors through the appropriate bureau.
  • Avoid applying to many lenders at once unless you need to compare formal offers.
  • Be accurate about existing loans and regular obligations.

Eligibility is not affordability

A lender may offer more than is comfortable for your own goals. Choose an EMI that still leaves room for emergencies, insurance, household costs and saving. Read our loan tenure guide to compare a lower EMI against the interest cost of a longer repayment period.
