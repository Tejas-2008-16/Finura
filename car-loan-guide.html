Car loan EMI: what to check before you buy

A car loan is often shorter than a home loan, so the EMI can be substantial even when the total interest is lower.

Start with the on-road price

Use the price you will actually pay, including registration, insurance, accessories and other relevant charges. Then subtract the down payment you can make without weakening your emergency savings.

Balance down payment and liquidity

A larger down payment reduces the financed amount and total interest. It should not leave you unable to cover insurance renewals, maintenance or unexpected expenses. Compare a few scenarios with the calculator.

Ask the lender these questions

  • Is the quoted rate fixed for the full term?
  • Are there processing, documentation or mandate charges?
  • Can you prepay, and are charges applicable?
  • What happens if an EMI is late?

Keep ownership costs separate

Fuel, servicing, parking and insurance are not part of the EMI. Add those to your monthly budget before deciding that a payment is affordable.
