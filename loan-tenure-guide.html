Borrowing basics

How to choose a loan tenure

The tenure changes both the monthly EMI and the interest paid over the life of the loan.

Shorter tenure

A shorter tenure typically means a higher EMI and lower total interest. It can be efficient when your income is stable and you retain a comfortable emergency buffer.

Longer tenure

A longer tenure reduces the monthly payment but normally adds interest. It can help preserve cash flow, particularly when you expect expenses or uncertain income.

Use a realistic budget

Test several tenures in the EMI calculator. Compare the EMI, total interest and repayment schedule, then choose a payment that leaves space for essential costs and saving.
